Intro

Developing relationships of trust with people who come from diverse backgrounds is my greatest strength. I became a software engineer because I love writing programs that provide creative solutions for everyday problems. Before becoming a programmer, I used Microsoft Excel to create these efficiencies. I now get to use JavaScript, React, Redux, Express, and more to build projects that mean something to me, and will provide value to customers and to a company. Take a look at some projects I have built!

Projects

GearAgain

GearAgain is an ecommerce site where users can buy and sell used outdoor gear. I built the application using JavaScript, React, and Redux on the front end, Express and Sequelize on the back end, and Postgres for the database. React components turned out to be extremely helpful because of the nature of needing to display identical product listings throughout the site.

I am particularly proud of the shopping cart feature. When a user adds items to their cart, a POST request is generated which posts the item to the database and updates the Redux store so that the items properly display in the cart. My goal with this approach (as opposed to using cookies or localstorage) was to ensure that the user's cart would persist across sessions and across devices.

The idea for GearAgain came to me as I watched my wife buy and resell used clothing, shoes, and other items online. Her experience with online sales, and our passion for the outdoors led me to build this platform. When people use my app, it encourages people to preserve this beautiful planet on which we live, and at the same time help others find quality outdoor gear for a great price.

OpenChef

OpenChef is an OpenTable clone where users can browse for and book chefs to come cook at their event. We utilized JavaScript, React, and Redux on the front end, and Python and Flask-SQLAlchemy on the back end with a PostgreSQL database.

My main focus was building a dynamic profile page which allows users to view and update their user and/or chef information. I utilized conditional logic and React state management to allow seamless toggling between Reservations and Account info.

I used thunk middleware and the built-in JavaScript fetch method to make a PATCH request to the back end where I wrote Python queries to update the user info in the PostgreSQL database.

Users can also view their chef reservations, past and current, which I styled using flexbox and I created the functionality on the front end and back end to both edit and delete a reservation.

Do Your Chores!

At some point, every parent wishes their kids would just stop playing for long enough to do their chores. I am such a parent, and getting my four rag-a-muffins to do their chores...is a chore.

The Do Your Chores! web application aims to solve the issue by allowing parents to sign in and add chores to different "Zones" in their house. Each chore is assigned to a child user who works to complete their list of assigned chores. Each time a chore is completed by a child, the parent will get prompted to approve the work. Once the chore is approved, the chore marks as complete, and the parent can say without any hesitation, "Yes, you can go play with friends."

The front end is built using React, Redux, and JavaScript and the backend uses Express, Sequelize, and PostgreSQL for the database. The app allows for two types of users: a parent, or account owner, and a child, who has limited access.

In future versions of this app, each time a child completes a chore they will make progress towards rewards that the parent and child set together.
